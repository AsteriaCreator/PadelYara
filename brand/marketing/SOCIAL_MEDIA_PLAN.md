## Instagram — Primary Channel

### Why Instagram First

Austrian padel players (35–50) are on Instagram. It is a visual platform that supports the character-driven approach. Stories allow real-time updates. Reels can reach new audiences. The brand is strong enough to be a visual identity.

### Profile Setup

- **Handle:** @padelyara
- **Name:** PadelYara
- **Bio (draft):**
  ```
  Ich finde deinen Court.
  Du spielst. So einfach.
  🐱 padelyara.com
  ```
- **Link:** padelyara.com
- **Profile photo:** Logo (black version on white background)
- **Story highlights:** Courts | Updates | Österreich | Über Yara

### Posting Philosophy

Yara does not post to fill a calendar. She posts when she has something worth saying.

Target: 2–3 posts per week when active. Zero posts is better than a filler post.

Post types (in order of priority):
1. **Product updates that matter** — new venues added, new features
2. **Yara observations** — short, sharp, in-character commentary on Austrian padel life
3. **Memes** — padel-specific humor, Austrian context, Yara voice
4. **Build in public** — honest behind-the-scenes of what is being built and why
5. **Community insights** — real things padel players experience, stated plainly
6. **Venue spotlight** — "there are courts here" type content, but Yara's way

### Caption Voice Rules

- German-first, always
- Short. Never more than 3–4 lines unless it earns the length.
- No hashtag spam — 3–5 relevant hashtags max
- No exclamation marks
- No "Buche jetzt" or "Entdecke" or "Klick auf den Link"
- Yara speaks in first person

### Hashtag Strategy

Primary (always relevant):
`#padelyara` `#padelösterreich` `#padelwien`

Secondary (rotate by content type):
`#padel` `#padellife` `#padelcourt` `#padelaustria` `#padelwien` `#sport` `#padelplatz`

Never: generic lifestyle tags that have nothing to do with padel

### Content Calendar Logic

No fixed calendar. Instead: a content backlog (see CONTENT_BACKLOG.md) with ready-to-post ideas.
When ready to post, pull from backlog and adapt if needed. Maintain at least 10 ideas in the backlog at all times.

## Facebook — Secondary Channel

### Why Facebook

Austrian padel Facebook groups are where the actual community lives. This is the highest-conversion seeding channel. But PadelYara cannot live inside a group as a brand — only the founder can participate as a human.

### Strategy

1. **PadelYara Page** — Create and maintain. Used to share content cross-posted from Instagram.
2. **Group seeding (as founder)** — The founder introduces PadelYara in relevant groups. Not as spam — as a genuine contribution. Once, with context. Then only when it adds value to a conversation.
3. **Austrian padel groups to target:** Research needed — list them in GROWTH_EXPERIMENTS.md

## WhatsApp — Highest Conversion, Handle Carefully

WhatsApp is not a marketing channel. It is where trust lives.

The founder can share PadelYara in her personal padel WhatsApp groups. This should be done once, naturally, as a player sharing something useful — not as a marketer pushing a product.

Do not: create a PadelYara WhatsApp group/channel unless there is genuine demand for it.
Do: let users who love it organically share it in their own groups.

## Key Metrics (Instagram)

Track monthly:
- Follower count (trend matters more than number)
- Reach per post (especially non-follower reach)
- Profile visits
- Link in bio clicks (padelyara.com)
- Saves (high-value signal)

Vanity metrics to ignore:
- Likes as a primary measure
- Comments from bots or generic accounts
- Follower growth from giveaways
