# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Urban Austrian padel players, predominantly 30–54. They play regularly and book through at least two different platforms (Eversports, eTennis, Tennis04). Their frustration is specific: too many tabs, too many booking sites, no single view of what's available. They're not beginners — they know the sport, they know the courts, they just need the friction removed. They use the site on mobile, often on the same day they want to play.

## Product Purpose

PadelYara aggregates padel court availability across Austria into one search. Users enter location, date, time, and duration — PadelYara returns what's free. Secondary features: Turnierjagd (tournament discovery + personal match analysis), Padelrevier (venue map). The measure of success is a user finding and booking a court in under two minutes, without opening another tab.

## Brand Personality

**Competent. Unbothered. Mean.**

Yara is a cat who fixed a problem she didn't ask to fix. She's not warm, not self-promotional, not trying to be liked. She states facts. She dismisses you the moment she's done helping. The character is the brand — everything should feel like it was built by someone who has very high standards and very low patience for inefficiency.

Three words: sharp, sovereign, dry.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS / boring competitors** — no "AI-powered", no blue gradient hero, no corporate-friendly tone. PadelYara has a voice; most court-booking tools don't.
- **Sports betting apps** — loud, cluttered, orange-on-dark, pushy CTAs, notification noise. The opposite of Yara's energy.
- **Austrian tourism sites** — alpine pastels, stock mountain photos, friendly-neutral copy, "Willkommen!" energy. This is not a Tourismusverband.
- **Minimal white startup** — beige/off-white, thin fonts, lots of whitespace-as-personality. The warmth has to come from voice and color, not absence.

## Design Principles

1. **The tool earns the voice.** Yara's character only works if the product actually delivers. Design should never let UI friction undermine the confidence the copy projects.
2. **Efficiency as aesthetic.** Nothing decorative unless it earns its place. Every element either informs or focuses — no padding for padding's sake.
3. **Coolness is restraint, not decoration.** The lime accent works because it's used sparingly. The dark background works because it's consistently dark. Adding more doesn't make it cooler.
4. **Mean ≠ confusing.** Dry copy and minimal UI are not licenses for unclear UX. Labels, states, and errors should be unmistakable even when terse.
5. **Austrian, not generic European.** The product is for Austria. City names, venue names, and interface copy should feel local and specific, not internationalized.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA — 4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text. Reduced-motion alternative required for any animation (crossfade or instant). Mobile-first: the primary device is a phone, often outdoors. Font sizes ≥16px on all inputs (already enforced). German-language interface throughout.
